Requirements Breakdown

Understanding FYC and submission requirements

üí∞ FYC Requirement: $2,500

First Year Commission (FYC) is the commission earned on new policies written during the Contract B period.

FYC Calculation

  • FYC is credited when policies are placed (paid for by client)
  • Cumulative total across all product lines
  • Must reach $2,500 by end of Contract B period
  • FYC from pending applications does not count until placed

Monthly Pace Targets

End of Month On-Track FYC % of Goal
Month 1 $625 25%
Month 2 $1,250 50%
Month 3 $1,875 75%
Month 4 $2,500 100%

üìù Submission Requirement: 5

Submissions are completed insurance applications submitted to the company for underwriting.

  • Minimum of 5 submissions required
  • Counts all product types
  • Submitted applications count even if ultimately declined
  • Quality matters - declined apps may indicate coaching needs
üí° Both Requirements Must Be Met An advisor must meet BOTH the $2,500 FYC AND 5 submissions to qualify for transition to Contract A. Meeting one but not the other is insufficient.

Understanding On Track, At Risk, and Critical status

üö¶ Status Color Coding

The system automatically calculates each advisor's status based on their progress relative to time elapsed:

‚úì On Track

Progress is equal to or ahead of time elapsed. Advisor is on pace to meet requirements.

‚ö† At Risk

Progress is behind schedule but still achievable with increased effort. Requires attention.

‚úï Critical

Significantly behind with limited time remaining. Urgent intervention needed or extension consideration.

Extensions Process

Granting extensions to Contract B advisors

‚è∞ Extension Policy

Contract B advisors who haven't met requirements may be granted an extension under certain conditions:

4
Max Extension Months
8
Max Total Months (4+4)

Extension Considerations:

  • Progress Made - Has the advisor shown improvement?
  • Extenuating Circumstances - Valid reasons for slow start?
  • Coaching Engagement - Has advisor been receptive to guidance?
  • Pipeline Quality - Pending applications likely to convert?

üìù Granting an Extension

  1. Open the advisor's Contract B record
  2. Click "Request Extension" or edit the End Date
  3. Enter extension length (1-4 months)
  4. Document justification in Notes field
  5. Submit for approval (if required by your org)
  6. System validates total doesn't exceed 8 months

‚ö†Ô∏è Maximum Extension Enforced The system will not allow extensions that would push the total Contract B period beyond 8 months (4 original + 4 extension maximum).

Outcomes & Actions

Handling Contract B completion scenarios

üéØ Possible Outcomes

‚úì Transition to Contract A

Advisor has met both requirements ($2,500 FYC and 5 submissions):

  • Update Contract B status to "Transitioned"
  • Create Contract A record
  • Congratulate advisor and announce to team

‚è≥ Extension Granted

Advisor shows promise but needs more time:

  • Document extension approval with reasons
  • Set clear expectations for extension period
  • Schedule additional coaching sessions
  • New end date reflects extension (max 4 additional months)

‚úï Termination

Advisor did not meet requirements and extension not warranted:

  • Follow HR termination procedures
  • Update Contract B status to "Terminated"
  • Document termination date and reason
  • Ensure proper offboarding

üìä Manager Responsibilities

Timeframe Action
Weekly Review Contract B dashboard, identify At Risk/Critical advisors
30 days before end Evaluate progress, decide on extension or outcome
At end date Process transition, extension, or termination
Monthly Report Contract B metrics to leadership
