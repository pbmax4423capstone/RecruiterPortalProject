‚ö†Ô∏è Important: The three required fields are Candidate, Interview Type, and Scheduled Date/Time. You cannot save the interview without these.
4

Fill Out Form Fields

Let's go through each field in detail:
Candidate REQUIRED
Type: Lookup (Searchable Dropdown)

Purpose: Select which candidate this interview is for. This is a searchable lookup field that connects the interview to an existing Candidate record.

How to use: Start typing the candidate's name and select from the dropdown list. The field searches across all active candidates in the system.

Example: Type "John Smith" and select the matching candidate from the list.

Validation: Must select an existing candidate. You cannot type in a name that doesn't exist in the system.

üí° Pro Tip: If you don't see the candidate you're looking for, they may not exist in the system yet. Create them first using the "Create New Candidate" button.
Interview Type REQUIRED
Type: Picklist (Dropdown)

Purpose: Indicates which stage of the interview process this is. This is critical for tracking candidate progression through the recruitment pipeline.

How to use: Select the appropriate interview type from the dropdown based on where the candidate is in the process.

Available Options:
  • Attraction Interview (Ci-First) - Initial contact call to gauge interest and explain opportunity. This is typically the first conversation with a candidate.
  • SI1 (Align-2nd) - First structured interview. Deeper discussion about role expectations, candidate background, and initial alignment assessment.
  • SI2 (Plan-3rd) - Second structured interview. Focus on career plans, compensation expectations, and business plan discussion.
  • SI3 (Present-4th) - Third structured interview. Final assessment, presentation of offer details, and addressing any remaining questions.
  • Career (Optional-5th) - Career presentation meeting. Optional additional meeting to present career path and long-term opportunities.
‚ö†Ô∏è Important: Select the correct interview type‚Äîit affects reporting and candidate stage tracking. The numbers (1st, 2nd, 3rd, 4th, 5th) indicate the typical sequence of interviews.
Scheduled Date/Time REQUIRED
Type: DateTime Picker

Purpose: The date and time when the interview will take place. This field sets when the interview is scheduled to occur.

How to use: Click the calendar icon to select a date, then use the time picker to set the exact time. The system will display times in your timezone.

Example: January 15, 2026 at 2:00 PM

Validation: Must be a future date and time. You cannot schedule interviews in the past.

üö´ Common Error: If you try to schedule an interview in the past, you'll receive a validation error. Always ensure the date/time is in the future.
üí° Best Practice: Schedule interviews with at least 24-48 hours notice to give candidates time to prepare and confirm availability.
Interviewer(s) OPTIONAL
Type: Text Field

Purpose: The name(s) of the person or people who will conduct the interview. This helps track who is interviewing which candidates.

How to use: Type the name(s) of the interviewer(s). If multiple people are interviewing, separate names with commas.

Example: Sarah Johnson, Michael Chen

Recommendation: Fill this in to ensure clarity on who owns the interview and who the candidate should expect to meet with.

üí° Tip: Recording interviewers helps with accountability and makes it easy to find interviews by who conducted them.
Notes OPTIONAL
Type: Long Text Area

Purpose: Capture any additional context, preparation notes, or special instructions for this specific interview.

How to use: Type any relevant notes that will help prepare for or conduct the interview effectively.

Example: "Focus on candidate's financial services background. Ask about willingness to relocate. Candidate mentioned interest in management track during screening call."

Recommendation: Use this field for anything you'll want to remember before or during the interview. These notes are visible on the interview record.

7

Next Steps: Completing Interviews

After the interview takes place, you need to mark it as complete. Navigate to the interview record and change the Status field from "Scheduled" to "Completed".
‚ö†Ô∏è Important Change: The system has been updated‚Äîyou NO LONGER need to manually enter a "Completed Date". When you change Status to "Completed", the system automatically sets the completed date to the current date/time. The old "Completed Date" field has been removed to simplify the process.
üí° Best Practice: Mark interviews complete as soon as possible after they occur. This keeps your metrics accurate and helps you track candidate progress in real-time.

‚ùå Common Mistakes to Avoid

Mistake #1: Confusing scheduled date/time with completed date.
Solution: The new system only has one date field‚ÄîScheduled Date/Time. When you mark Status as "Completed", the system automatically records when it was completed. You don't need to worry about completed dates anymore.
Mistake #2: Not marking interviews complete after they happen.
Solution: Always update the Status field to "Completed" after conducting an interview. This keeps your dashboard metrics accurate.
Mistake #3: Selecting the wrong interview type.
Solution: Pay attention to the interview stage‚Äîselecting "SI2" when it should be "SI1" affects reporting and candidate progression tracking.
Mistake #4: Scheduling interviews in the past.
Solution: The system prevents this with validation. Scheduled Date/Time must be in the future. If you conducted an interview already, create it with a future date then immediately mark it complete.
Mistake #5: Creating multiple interviews for the same stage.
Solution: Each candidate should only have one interview per type (one "SI1", one "SI2", etc.). If you need to reschedule, edit the existing interview record rather than creating a duplicate.

üìã Quick Reference Summary

  1. Click "Schedule Interview" button (5th button in header)
  2. Select Candidate from searchable lookup (required)

  4. Set Scheduled Date/Time using date/time picker (required) - Must be future date
  5. Add Interviewer(s) name(s) (optional but recommended)
  6. Add Notes for context (optional but helpful)
  7. Click "Schedule Interview" button
  8. Verify interview appears in dashboard scheduled count
  9. After interview occurs, change Status to "Completed" (no completed date needed)
