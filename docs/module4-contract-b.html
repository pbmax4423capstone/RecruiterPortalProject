What is Contract B?

Understanding the Contract B probationary period

üìã Contract B Overview

Contract B is a 4-month probationary period for new agents. During this time, agents must demonstrate their ability to meet production requirements before transitioning to a full Contract A.

Requirements Overview

What agents must achieve during Contract B

üìä Contract B Requirements

To successfully complete Contract B and transition to Contract A, agents must meet both requirements:

Requirement Target How It's Measured
FYC (First Year Commission) $2,500 Sum of FYC from all policies written during Contract B period
Submissions 5 policies Count of policy submissions (not issued policies)

‚è±Ô∏è Timeline

The 4-month clock starts from the agent's Contract B Start Date:

Month 1

Onboarding, training, initial prospecting

Month 2

Active selling, first submissions expected

Month 3

Building momentum, FYC accumulating

Month 4

Final push to meet requirements

‚ö†Ô∏è Both Requirements Must Be Met An agent with $3,000 FYC but only 3 submissions has NOT completed Contract B. Both thresholds must be reached.

Status Indicators

Understanding On Track, At Risk, and Critical statuses

üè∑Ô∏è Status Badge Definitions

The system automatically calculates and displays a status badge based on the agent's progress relative to time remaining:

On Track

Performing Well

FYC and submissions are on pace to meet requirements before deadline

At Risk

Behind Pace

Currently behind on one or both metrics; needs attention

Critical

Significantly Behind

Unlikely to meet requirements without extension or immediate improvement

üìà Status Calculation Logic

Status is determined by comparing actual progress to expected progress:

Status FYC Progress Submission Progress
On Track ‚â•75% of expected pace ‚â•75% of expected pace
At Risk 50-74% of expected pace 50-74% of expected pace
Critical <50% of expected pace <50% of expected pace

Example: At month 2 (50% of time elapsed), an "On Track" agent should have ~$1,250 FYC and ~2.5 submissions.

Step 7 of 8

Best Practices

Tips for effective Contract B management

‚úÖ Contract B Management Best Practices

  • Review dashboard weekly - Don't wait until month 4 to identify problems
  • Act on "At Risk" early - Reach out to agents falling behind
  • Document coaching - Record all support and guidance provided
  • Set expectations clearly - Ensure agents understand the $2,500 FYC and 5 submission requirements
  • Celebrate successes - Acknowledge agents who transition to Contract A

‚ùå Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Ignoring "Critical" status - These agents need immediate attention
  • Late extensions - Don't wait until the last day to grant extensions
  • Missing documentation - Always document decisions and rationale
  • Incorrect FYC tracking - Ensure all policies are properly linked
  • Forgetting submission counts - FYC alone doesn't complete Contract B

üìÖ Weekly Review Checklist

  • ‚òê Review Contract B dashboard
  • ‚òê Check agents with <30 days remaining
  • ‚òê Follow up with "At Risk" and "Critical" agents
  • ‚òê Verify FYC totals are current
  • ‚òê Document coaching conversations
  • ‚òê Plan transitions for agents meeting requirements
üí° Pro Tip Set a calendar reminder for the 15th and end of each month to review all Contract B agents and take action before deadlines.
