Interview Types

Understanding the different interview stages

üéØ Interview Type Definitions

Candidates progress through multiple interview stages. Select the appropriate type when scheduling:

üìû Initial Screen

First contact - typically a 15-30 minute phone or video call to assess basic fit and interest.

üëî Manager Interview

Meeting with the hiring manager to discuss role details, experience, and team fit.

üéØ Final Interview

Final round with senior leadership or panel. Often includes presentation or case study.

üìã Technical Assessment

Skills evaluation specific to the role. May include tests, exercises, or demonstrations.

üìä Typical Interview Flow

Stage Duration Conducted By Focus Areas
Initial Screen 15-30 min Recruiter Interest, availability, basic qualifications
Manager Interview 45-60 min Hiring Manager Experience, skills, culture fit
Final Interview 60-90 min Leadership Team Strategic thinking, leadership, values
üí° Pro Tip Not every candidate requires all interview types. Skip stages when appropriate based on role level and candidate experience.

Scheduling Interviews

Learn how to schedule interviews for candidates

üìÖ How to Schedule an Interview

Use the "Schedule Interview" action button in the dashboard header to launch the scheduling flow.

üì∏
Screenshot: Schedule Interview Button

Scheduling Flow Steps:

  1. Select Candidate - Search and choose the candidate
  2. Choose Interview Type - Select from available types
  3. Set Date & Time - Pick the interview date and time
  4. Add Details - Location, video link, notes
  5. Confirm & Save - Review and create the interview

Date/Time Yes When the interview is scheduled

Recording Completion

How to mark interviews as completed and record outcomes

‚úÖ Marking Interviews Complete

After an interview occurs, you must update the record to reflect completion. Use the "Log Interview" action button or edit the interview record directly.

üì∏
Screenshot: Log Interview Action

Interview Statuses

Scheduled Completed Cancelled

üìù Completion Checklist

When marking an interview as completed, update these fields:

  • Status - Change from "Scheduled" to "Completed"
  • Actual Date - Confirm or adjust if rescheduled
  • Outcome Notes - Record key observations
  • Next Steps - What happens next for this candidate

Automatic Updates

When you complete an interview:

  • Candidate's "Highest Level Achieved" updates automatically
  • Interview statistics on dashboard refresh
  • Pipeline metrics recalculate
üí° Complete Same Day Update interview status on the same day it occurs for accurate metrics. Delayed updates affect daily/weekly reports.

Best Practices

Tips for effective interview management

‚úÖ Interview Scheduling Best Practices

  • Schedule promptly - Create interview records as soon as confirmed
  • Include all details - Video links, locations, preparation notes
  • Confirm with candidates - Send calendar invites immediately
  • Buffer time - Allow 15 minutes between interviews
  • Update immediately - Mark completed same day

‚ùå Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Forgetting to update status - Leaves metrics inaccurate
  • Wrong interview type - Skews reporting by stage
  • Missing outcome notes - Loses valuable context
  • Backdating completions - Distorts timeline metrics
  • Deleting cancelled interviews - Mark as Cancelled instead

üéØ Interview Quality Checklist

Before moving to the next stage, verify:

  • ‚òê Interview scheduled with correct type
  • ‚òê Candidate received calendar invite
  • ‚òê Video link or location confirmed
  • ‚òê Interview marked complete after occurrence
  • ‚òê Outcome notes recorded
  • ‚òê Next steps identified
üí° Pro Tip Set a daily reminder to review and update any interviews from the previous day. This keeps metrics accurate and pipeline current.
